How it Works?

Step 1: Prepare for the Retrospective

Schedule the retrospective session and invite team members to attend. Choose a suitable retrospective technique or format for the session.

Step 2: Set the Stage

Begin the retrospective by setting the stage. Welcome participants and explain the purpose of the retrospective. Remind everyone of the retrospective's guiding principles and the importance of open and honest communication.

Step 3: Gather Data

Start collecting data by asking team members to share their observations and feedback on the recent iteration or project. Use techniques like "Start-Stop-Continue to collect insights.

Step 4: Generate Insights

Facilitate a discussion around the collected data to identify patterns, trends, and recurring themes. Encourage team members to explore the reasons behind both positive outcomes and challenges.

Step 5: Identify Improvement Actions

Guide the team in generating actionable improvement ideas based on the identified insights. Prioritize the improvement actions that will have the most significant impact on the team's processes and collaboration.

Step 6: Decide What to Do

Collaboratively decide on the specific actions the team will take to address the identified improvements. Assign responsibilities and due dates for each action item.

Step 7: Close the Retrospective

Summarize the key findings, decisions, and action items from the retrospective. Reinforce the commitment of the team to implement the agreed-upon improvements.

Step 8: Implement Action Items

Following the retrospective, team members work on implementing the assigned action items. Regularly track progress on the action items and discuss any challenges during subsequent retrospectives.

Step 9: Reflect and adapt

In future retrospectives, reflect on the progress made based on the implemented improvements. Assess the impact of the changes and identify further areas for refinement and growth.
